Sustainability Research

Explaining the foundational studies and agreements that shape our understanding of sustainable development.

Our Common Future (Brundtland Report)

Published: 1987

Published by the World Commission on Environment and Development (WCED), this landmark report introduced the modern definition of "sustainable development": meeting present needs without compromising future generations.

Read Report

The Limits to Growth

Published: 1972

A groundbreaking study commissioned by the Club of Rome. It used computer modeling to predict the consequences of exponential economic and population growth with finite resource supplies, sparking global debate on sustainability.

Read More

The Paris Agreement

Adopted: 2015

A legally binding international treaty on climate change adopted at COP21. Its goal is to limit global warming to well below 2, preferably to 1.5 degrees Celsius, compared to pre-industrial levels.

Read Agreement

Agenda 21

Published: 1992

A comprehensive plan of action to be taken globally, nationally, and locally by organizations of the United Nations System, Governments, and Major Groups in every area in which humans impact the environment.
